# Studies (n > 1)

Multi-subject studies and citizen-science protocols.

Distinguished from `case-studies/` (which hold n=1 personal case reports) in that these:
- Recruit multiple participants
- Have a pre-registered hypothesis and protocol
- Include consent / data-handling / anonymization standards from the start
- Are designed to generate hypothesis-level evidence across a small cohort, not just a personal narrative

## Why "citizen science"?

Large formal trials cost millions and take years. For hypothesis-generating pilot work in conditions where the evidence base is thin — and endometriosis is one of those — **structured, methodologically transparent citizen science** can bridge the gap. Participants self-collect at-home test results (Evvy for microbiome, WHOOP for physiology), share de-identified data under explicit consent, and the pooled analysis is published with the methodology open to critique.

This is the model the [Isala Project](https://isala.be) (Lebeer lab, University of Antwerp, 2023) used to map the healthy vaginal microbiome across ~3,345 women, and the model the [American Gut Project](https://microsetta.ucsd.edu/american-gut-project/) (Knight lab, UC San Diego) used to build a gut-microbiome reference. Neither replaced the need for RCTs. Both generated hypotheses now being tested formally.

## Data handling standards

Every study in this directory commits to the following, enforced via [`disclosures.md`](../disclosures.md):

1. **No PHI in the repo.** Ever. No names, DOB, addresses, physician identifiers, or test IDs.
2. **Explicit opt-in consent per data use.** Participants can share and un-share at any time.
3. **Aggregate-first analysis.** Individual-level data is only published with explicit consent.
4. **Research-ethics alignment.** We aim to conform to the principles of the Declaration of Helsinki for observational research and publish enough methodological detail for external review.
5. **Not a clinical trial.** No interventions are prescribed; participants bring their own protocols.
